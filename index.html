Economics and Business Student Association
The Economics and Business Student Association (EBSA) is one of the largest undergraduate professional business organization at UC Davis. Our community is built on our membersâ€™ active participation and passion for business. We all work hard together to grow our individual skills. As we develop and build strong lasting friendships, we strengthen our EBSA community. Our mission at EBSA is to provide resources in order to build a community of future business leaders and to be the bridge linking your undergraduate and professional career. We are a happy and sustainable community that helps one another strengthen our individual skills and turn our conceptual knowledge into practical skills.
Professionalism
Through resume building, professional workshops, mentoring, and networking opportunities, you will increase your professional knowledge, competence, and essential skills to excel in the business world and develop your personal brand.
Community
We acknowledge and value our community. We recognize that by working together we can accomplish more. For this reason, EBSA regularly holds socials and networking events where you can get connected with like minded individuals to help you grow your community.
Discovery
At EBSA, you are here to clarify your personal and professional purpose. We specialize in helping you discover your passion and creating opportunities to steer you in the right direction. We give you a network of purpose-driven leaders to help you achieve your goals.

Member Spotlight

It's time for a member spotlight and this week we have...


David Miller!


This past summer, David was able to work as a Supply Chain intern at Unilever. He says,


""From working alongside different managers within the Supply Chain department I was able to understand the strategies and networks involved in managing one of the best supply chains in the world. I was given the opportunity to visit multiple warehouses and be involved in numerous planning team meetings whereby I learned how different stakeholders within the ecosystem interacted and ways to adapt to changes in sales, inventory, and production in an efficient manner.


It was truly amazing to be a part of a purpose-driven community that fostered development both internally and in society through its strong efforts in building highly sustainable and impactful brands.


My favorite project I worked on was building a new logistics cost tracker and network map. This involved sourcing data from each distributor and creating an excel template and dashboard. This showcased changes in costs and volume week on week, segmented by distributor and region. Ultimately this allowed for various logistics costs to be traced more accurately (and for new rates to be updated easily.)"


Way to go, David!

Member Requirements:
As a member of EBSA, you must complete the following requirements during the quarter in order to retain membership status.
1. Pay a $30 membership fee. (Venmo or cash)
2. Attend 2 socials.
3. Attend 2 guest speaker events.
4. Earn 1 fundraising credit.
5. Miss no more than 2 general meetings.
6. Participate in the quarterly case competition.
